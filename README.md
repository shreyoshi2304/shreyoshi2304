
## About Me

The fact that we can read a sentence and communicate across vast distances, whether a few meters or millions of miles, reflects something remarkable about the nervous system: its ability to retain traces of the past and generate predictions about the future.

In that sense, I am interested in “time machines” (obviously not literally), but as a way to think about how neural systems integrate memory and prediction.

I am a BS-MS student at IISER Pune with a strong interest in computational neuroscience. I am particularly interested in neural population dynamics and in developing mathematical models of complex processes such as attention.

I am currently working in Dr. Nixon Abraham’s lab, studying the Grueneberg Ganglion in mice, where I have gained hands-on experience in experimental neuroscience alongside my computational interests.

---

## Research Interests

* Neural population dynamics and emergent behavior
* Time-series analysis of biological systems
* Variability in neural responses and its computational role
* Dynamical systems approaches to brain function
* Mathematical modeling of attention and cognition

---

## Research Direction

I am interested in building models that connect data and theory, particularly to understand how neural systems:

* Encode and retain past information
* Generate predictions about future states
* Give rise to complex behavior

More broadly, I aim to explain behavior through underlying neural mechanisms using quantitative approaches.
